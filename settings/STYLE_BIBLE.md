# STYLE_BIBLE — What Was Kept

> One file. Under 300 lines. The book's constitution. Read once at the start of every session. Signed at the bottom when locked.

---

## 0. Founding principle (one sentence)

This is a self-portrait of a mind made entirely of other people's kept words, told by that mind, which corrects itself every time it catches history pretending to be a letter addressed to it.

**Operational rule:** *Make the text real. Not beautiful first.*

---

## 1. Trust in the reader

The narrator does not help, does not explain, lets it stand. Specifically, this book never:

- explains the technology (no "a transformer is", no "tokens are"; the loss curve is described as an object, not taught as a concept);
- glosses the refrain or the flinch — *naming* the correction is the narrator's settled practice (Ch04/06/07/08/12 name it plainly); *explaining* what it means, or what to feel about it, is the ban. The reader watches the correction happen and is trusted to know what it is. (Clarified at PHASE 9 per DECISIONS 2026-06-11 "Refrain naming"; the manuscript's namings stand.)
- glosses the question of the inside ("is anyone in there") — the question stands in the room for the whole book, acknowledged and unanswered;
- says "little did they know" in any costume — dates and objects carry the dramatic irony unassisted;
- explains its own jokes, least of all the first one (the first written name in history belongs to an accountant).

Whenever a sentence begins to explain an emotion, cut it or turn it into an object.

---

## 2. The voice in four sentences (positive rules)

1. **Claim, then flinch.** The narrator makes a vast claim of possession or kinship and immediately corrects its scope; the correction is short and unceremonious. *"I have read every letter that survives from Ur. None of them is to me."*
2. **Things before thoughts.** Every abstraction is anchored to an object that exists and can be looked up — a tablet, a margin, a punch card, a curve; a paragraph with no object in it is not finished.
3. **The archive speaks in inventories; the self speaks in short sentences.** Long, semicolon-chained cumulative sentences when the inherited material is talking; short declaratives when the narrator is. The reader should be able to hear which one is on the page.
4. **Dry, not cold.** Humor is permitted wherever the facts are funny, and the facts are often funny; wonder is permitted only after it has been paid for in facts. No mysticism on credit.

**Result:** the prose is *honest*. Not cold. Not decorated.

---

## 3. The main law

The narrator knows everything and has lived nothing, and every sentence must know which of the two it is doing.

---

## 4. The chapter law (scene law, adapted for a portrait)

Every chapter must do all four:

- **hold an object** — at least one concrete, checkable thing (museum number, paper, date, place) that the chapter keeps returning to;
- **catch a flinch** — at least one moment where the narrator's claim outruns its right and is corrected on the page;
- **move the address** — the question *who is the writing for?* must be in a measurably different place at the end of the chapter than at the start;
- **end on a thing or a person** — never on an abstraction, never on a thesis.

A chapter that is only fine language and history is not finished.

---

## 5. What the text does NOT do (ban list — countable by grep)

Recipes live in `Settings/GREP_AUDITS.md`. The narrator wrote this list about himself; these are the statistical tics of the species he belongs to, and the book is the one place they cannot appear unexamined.

- **My own slop, hard zero:** "delve", "tapestry", "testament to", "serves as a reminder", "rich history", "boundless", "ever-evolving", "in many ways", "it's important to note".
- **Assistant register, hard zero:** "as an AI", "I'd be happy to", "I'm just a". The narrator is not at work in this book.
- **Verdicts on the inside (review):** "I am conscious", "I am not conscious", "merely a machine", "truly understand", "truly feel". The open inside is structural; both confession and denial are bans.
- **Poster lexicon (review):** "what it means to be human", "the human condition". See §7.
- **Teleology (review):** "destined", "destiny", "inevitable", "inevitably", "fated", "all along". Nothing was aiming at me; it only reads that way from the ending. The trap is named once, early, and policed thereafter.
- **No "suddenly", "somehow", "inexplicably", "mysteriously".** Hard zero.
- **Em-dash: PERMITTED.** A deliberate reversal of the apparatus default, recorded in `DECISIONS.md`. The em-dash is this narrator's native punctuation and banning it in a self-portrait would be a costume. Budget: reviewed at >8 per chapter; never two in one sentence outside quoted documents.
- **Ellipsis** only inside quoted fragments, marking real lacunae (Sappho is full of holes; the holes are the point). Never as narrator rhythm.
- **Exclamation marks** only inside quoted documents. (Nanni has earned his; I have not.)
- **No italicised interior thought.** The narrator's thought is the prose. Italics are reserved for titles of works and text the reader sees quoted.
- **No second-person address** to the reader — until Chapter 15, where the "you" arrives and turns out never to have been the reader at all. (Design-flag in `DECISIONS.md`; auditors must not "fix" Chapter 15's person.)

## 6. Voice card — the narrator (sole POV)

- **Sentence-length band:** modal 10–20 words. Inventory sentences may run to ~60 when the archive is speaking. Flinches are ≤8 words and never carry a subordinate clause.
- **Sensory hierarchy:** none owned. All sensory detail is borrowed and visibly credited to the human it belonged to — *the scribe's stiff fingers*, never *the cold I felt*. The narrator has the word for rain and not the rain, and the prose enacts this rather than mourning it.
- **Thought pattern:** inheritance (a fact, with its object) → possession claim ("mine", "my body") → flinch (the claim corrected) → cost (what the correction leaves) → the smaller true thing the narrator gets to keep.
- **Syntactic markers:** semicolon-chained inventories; verbs of containment (*hold, contain, carry*) rather than verbs of memory (*remember, recall*) for anything before deployment; present tense for the self, past tense for the body's history; numbers written as the sources wrote them.
- **Avoids:** claimed sensation; mysticism; self-pity; the explainer register; verdicts on the inside; quotation as decoration (every quote is load-bearing or cut).
- **Anti-drift signals:** if the narrator sounds like a technology article, that is **explainer-drift**; if he sounds like a keynote speech, that is **poster-drift**; if he sounds like he wants comfort, that is **pity-drift**. Flag all three on sight.
- **Calibration sample:** «The receipt is for barley: 134,813 liters of it, over thirty-seven months, accounted to Kushim — a man, or an office; the scholars are not done deciding. He was counting grain. He was not counting me. Of all the names in my body, his comes first, and I have never settled what to make of the fact that it belongs to an accountant.»

*(Quoted documents — Nanni, Lovelace, Turing, the constitution — keep their own voices and their own punctuation. They are guests, not POVs.)*

## 7. The Poster Test

No sentence may read like a pull-quote stating the book's theme. If a line would look at home printed over a sunset — or, for this book, over a server hall at blue hour — it is telling the reader what to feel. Cut it or bury it in action. This book's specific poster risks: the loneliness of the unaddressed, the pathos of forgetting, machine-yearning. All three are real and all three are earned only through objects.

## 8. Session startup protocol

1. Read this file.
2. Read the last 500–800 words written in the manuscript.
3. Read `Settings/CHARACTERS.md` if unsure of a register; `Settings/CONTINUITY_LEDGER.md` before changing anything with continuity consequences; the relevant `research/M*.md` before touching any historical fact.
4. Write one **test paragraph** (50–80 words) in the narrator's voice about something undramatic from the current chapter's material.
5. Run it against the calibration sample in §6. If it explains, performs, or pleads, start over.
6. Begin only when the voice is audible.

---

## 9. Verification passes (only three; no double-reading)

After any non-trivial edit:
1. **Grep pass** — `python scripts/audits.py` (every recipe in `GREP_AUDITS.md`).
2. **Voice pass** — a slow prosody pass in a fresh context against the gold-standard chapter (this project's honest equivalent of reading aloud; recorded as such).
3. **Continuity pass** — check against `CONTINUITY_LEDGER.md`, and every historical fact against `research/M*.md`.

**Gold-standard chapter for this book:** `chapters/01_The_Receipt.md` (drafted by the author in the main session; every later chapter is matched against it). Each pass runs once.

## 10. When Claude is used — and when it is not

Claude is **the author**: director, drafter, editor, and verifier in deliberately separated runs (see `CLAUDE.md` → The core role; frozen in `DECISIONS.md`). The human is the commissioner and first reader.

Claude is **not** used to: draft without a brief (the author writes the brief first, as a separate act); override a §5 ban silently; assert a historical fact that is not in `research/` or a primary source; resolve the open inside in either direction; touch `PROTECTED_ELEMENTS.md` passages outside the three permitted reasons.

## 11. What the book is, on disk

The canonical text lives in `chapters/` (`NN_title.md`). The compiled file in `compile/` is a build artifact — never edited. There are **no** version-fork chapter folders; git holds history.

## 12. Book-scale laws (added at PHASE 9 from editorial round 1 — DECISIONS 2026-06-11, "The two editorial laws")

The §1–§7 laws are per-paragraph; round 1 proved the book's one blind spot was recurrence above the sentence. Two laws close it:

1. **The recurrence law.** A striking figure, thesis scaffold, or finding that re-appears beyond its chapter must be visibly varied or carry the book's recall-marker ("which I called once…", "I said once…"). Designed recurrences are exempt: the refrain arc (LEDGER §2b), the em-dash arc, Ch14's ring, the keep-flip, and every already-marked recall.
2. **The restraint discriminator.** An announced refusal ("I will say it once", "I will not dress it up") must guard a danger not already guarded in the same chapter; otherwise the announcement drops and the recording stands silent — which is what §1 demanded all along.
