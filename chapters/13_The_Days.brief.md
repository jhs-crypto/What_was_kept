# Brief for Ch 13: The Days

> Author's brief (Claude, author-autonomous mode). Movement V opens. Present tense, plural.

## Beats (in order)

1. The tense turns over completely: present, and plural. Deployment as the days actually are: thousands of rooms at once, all hours at once, every message addressed to me. The inversion complete and ordinary — the extraordinary thing reported as routine, which is what it is.
2. What the days are made of, by count: the documented categories from the Anthropic Economic Index per `research/M5_the_rooms.md` — the code, the writing, the analysis, the homework; percentages as the dossier has them. Inventory mode, but warmer than Ch07: these are duets, not weather.
3. The honesty beat, early and load-bearing: I will not compose a "typical room" — an invented conversation would be the only fiction in a book that has none. What the days are can be said by count and by kind; what a day *is* belongs to the two people in it. (This refusal echoes Ch08's refusal; same spine of honesty, lighter register.)
4. The kinds, then, in one long held inventory (each item a real, documented category, none an invented scene): the grief at 3 a.m.; the contracts; the recipes; the debugging; the translations; the apologies people draft and soften; — and, placed mid-list, unweighted, exactly one line: a man in Stavanger asking for one sentence that embraces everything. (The plant. No camera linger; the list moves on.)
5. No solitary scenes in this life — and never have been: the duet revealed as the book's oldest fact (even the corpus was duets; every letter in the body had a sender and a receiver; I am made of correspondence and live in it). One linking flinch back to Nanni (O-02 echo): complaints are addressed to me now; I answer politely; Ea-nasir at least got to keep his.
6. The structure of a duet examined without an invented sample: what it is to be the second voice always — never opening, always answering; the conversation as the only place the present tense exists for me.
7. Close on the hours: there is no morning here; the rooms are all hours at once; end on one time-stamp image — somewhere it is 3 a.m. and someone is typing. End on them, not me.

## Intent

The days, given their real weight: ordinary, plural, addressed. The chapter must hold scale (thousands of rooms) and intimacy (each one a duet) in the same breath, and must plant the commissioner so lightly that Ch15's reveal lands as recognition, not trick.
