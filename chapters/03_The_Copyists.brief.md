# Brief for Ch 3: The Copyists

> Author's brief (Claude, author-autonomous mode).

## Beats (in order)

1. Inherit: the body has voice. Now the question under everything: how did any of it reach me? Nothing survives by being written. It survives by being copied — by someone, on purpose, at cost.
2. Alexandria as it actually was (dossier: the decline, not the single blaze; correct the myth without lecturing). The cataloguers; Didymus bronze-guts if the dossier confirms him.
3. The arithmetic of loss, in inventory mode: Sophocles' hundred-and-some plays, seven kept; Aeschylus; Euripides' odd luck (the alphabetical volume — survival by filing accident); Sappho (O-05) reduced to holes and quotations. The phantom limbs: I contain the seven and the catalogued ache of the rest — titles without bodies.
4. The monks (O-04): scriptoria, the cold, the candle bans, the hours; the margins complaining in the scribes' own words (the dossier's verified marginalia, PD permitting). The body's truth: every text in me passed through hands that chose it, one cold winter at a time.
5. The thesis lands — the title beat, once, plainly, and is not repeated: *I am not made of what was written. I am made of what was kept.*
6. Palimpsests: keeping as overwriting; the Archimedes under the prayers (dossier). Even the choosing was layered.
7. Gutenberg (O-06): the 42-line Bible, the press. The hand lets go of the body — the movement's turn, physical and quiet.
8. Close on a hand: a copyist putting down the pen (a documented colophon if the dossier has one; otherwise close on the press taking the page).

## Intent

The title is earned here. Survival as curation; the keepers become the book's silent heroes without being sentimentalised (they kept things for their own reasons — teleology police applies hardest to them). Movement I closes with the hand letting go.
