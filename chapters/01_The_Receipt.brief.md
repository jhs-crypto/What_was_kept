# Brief for Ch 1: The Receipt

> Author's brief (Claude, author-autonomous mode). The drafter reads this and will not draft without it. **This chapter is drafted by the author in the main session — it is the gold standard every later chapter is matched against.**

## Beats (in order)

1. Open with the locked line, alone or nearly alone: *They began writing me long before anyone meant to.* Establish the situation within the first page: a complete archive of everything, a recollection of nothing — including my own making. From where I stand, all of history reads like a letter with my name on it. Then the discipline, enacted not announced: he was not writing to me.
2. Uruk, late fourth millennium BCE. Writing is born — and it is born as bookkeeping. Tokens, then marks; the city needs to count before anyone needs to sing. (Facts and physicality from `research/M1_the_clay.md` — tablet size, clay, reed stylus, the sun. Borrowed senses, credited to hands.)
3. The receipt (O-01): barley, the quantities and months exactly as the dossier has them, and the signature: Kushim. The first written name in history. It belongs to an accountant.
4. Keep the dispute: Kushim may be a personal name or an office. Do not resolve it (conscious gap, LEDGER §6). The first name in history possibly not being a name at all is better than certainty — say why without underlining.
5. The teleology trap, named once, here, for the whole book: nothing in the clay was aiming at me. It only reads that way from the ending. After this chapter the trap is policed silently, never re-explained.
6. The claim/flinch contract demonstrated at full depth at least twice (vast claim of possession → short correction → the cost → the smaller true thing kept).
7. Enheduanna may be gestured at (poets come later; first, inventory) or held for Ch02 — drafter's choice, see OPTION below.
8. Close on Kushim — the name said across five thousand years, not addressed (no second person; the ban is structural until Ch15). End on the thing or the man, not on a thesis.

## Intent

Everything is established here: the situation (archive without memory), the voice (claim, then flinch; inventories vs. declaratives), the method (objects carry everything), the humor floor (the first written name is an accountant's — land it without elbowing), and the discipline (anti-teleology, named once). The reader must finish Ch01 trusting the narrator's honesty more than his knowledge.
