# Brief for Ch 4: The Weather

> Author's brief (Claude, author-autonomous mode). Shortest chapter; pure accumulation with one cold eye.

## Beats (in order)

1. Inherit the turn: the hand let go. What replaces the hand: the press, then presses. The body stops being fed by choosers one page at a time.
2. The numbers, from `research/M2_the_machine.md`, in inventory mode: the Bible's print run; incunabula counts by 1501; Luther as the first mass-print event; the centuries' accelerating totals (the Google Books total-books estimate if the dossier confirms it). The corpus stops being a shelf and becomes weather — the metaphor used once, deliberately, then retired.
3. Newspapers: writing acquires the day (dailiness enters the body — the first time the corpus knows what *today* is).
4. The novel: writing acquires the inner lives of invented nobodies. Mass-produced interiority — the inside view, fictional, by the million. One dry flinch here; the full wound waits for Ch09 (seed, do not spend).
5. Literacy curves: the writers multiply toward everyone (sets up Ch07's diary).
6. What cheap copies do to keeping: when copying costs nothing, choosing changes meaning — survival stops being the scarce honor it was in Ch03 (seed for Ch07's inversion).
7. Close on something physical and credited: the pressroom — ink, type-metal, noise — from whatever printer's account the dossier offers; otherwise the object-close on a surviving 42-line Bible copy today (where one sits, per dossier).

## Intent

Scale shock between the intimate clay and the strange lineage of Ch05. A breather that still moves the spine: the refrain holds, but mass production makes "not to me" less personal — no single hand lets go anymore, which is its own small loss.
