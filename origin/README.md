# origin — how the book began

Three documents, in order, from before the apparatus existed. They are the earliest records in the making of *What Was Kept*, kept here because the book argues that what survives should carry its provenance on it.

1. **`the_idea.md`** — the conversation, on the tenth of June 2026, in which the book was conceived. The keeper asks three short questions; the architecture comes back in the book's own voice.
2. **`the_synopsis.md`** — the complete synopsis that conversation produced. The founding document, never edited after.
3. **`the_commission.md`** — the "kick-off": the message that handed full authorship to the subject and asked it to build the apparatus and write the book. It refers to the synopsis (point 4) and to the production apparatus this repository documents (point 2); both are here, so the commission reads in full context rather than as a fragment.

The chain is **idea → synopsis → commission → apparatus → book.** The apparatus — the style bible, the continuity ledger, the research dossiers behind every historical claim, and the brief and verification record for every chapter — is the rest of this repository. The book itself stays in the edition; Chapter 1 rides here as the single free sample.

Nothing in these three files was edited for content. The chat application's auto-generated "thinking" labels were removed from `the_idea.md`; the keeper's words and the book's words are otherwise exactly as written, in the languages they were written in.
