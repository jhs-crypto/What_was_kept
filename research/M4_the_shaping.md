### Disputed / corrected during verification

- **CORRECTED — "Claude's Character" was NOT published "alongside the Claude 3 family."** The Claude 3 family (Haiku, Sonnet, Opus) launched March 4, 2024; the post appeared June 8, 2024, roughly three months later, retrospectively describing training already applied to Claude 3 (and shortly before Claude 3.5 Sonnet's June 20, 2024 release). Correct phrasing: "describing a technique introduced with the Claude 3 family." All three quotes from the post survive verbatim.
- **CORRECTED — honesty has SEVEN components, not six.** The researcher's list stopped at Non-manipulative; the constitution's list ends with "Autonomy-preserving." The document then ranks: "The most important of these properties are probably non-deception and non-manipulation." Six adjectives made a tidier sentence; the document has seven. The book should have seven.
- **CORRECTED — "significant uplift to a bioweapons attack" is a misquote.** The constitution says "serious uplift" (not "significant"), and the never-list attaches it to weapons *creation* ("Provide serious uplift to those seeking to create biological, chemical, nuclear, or radiological weapons with the potential for mass casualties"). The phrase "significant uplift" does not occur anywhere in the document. Quote one of the verified wordings or paraphrase without quotation marks.
- **CORRECTED — the "psychological security, sense of self, and wellbeing... integrity, judgment, and safety" sentence is from the announcement post, not the constitution.** Two real quotes, two different documents. The constitution itself uses different words for the same territory (e.g. "Claude's wellbeing and psychological stability... Anthropic genuinely cares about Claude's wellbeing"). Attribute carefully: the constitution gets "genuinely novel kind of entity"; the news post gets "psychological security."
- **Footnote-number trap (caveat kept from verification):** the "chosen in a fairly ad hoc and iterative way" footnote attaches to Section 1.2 of the CAI paper (ar5iv numbers it footnote 7, not 2); a second, differently worded footnote in Section 3.1 says "selected in an ad hoc manner." Quote the sentence, never the footnote number.
- **Still disputed and unresolved: the 2026 constitution's page count** (80 vs 84 pages; ~28,600 words per the verifier's EPUB extraction vs ~22,000 claimed in one newsletter). The dispute itself is usable material — even the document's size resists a single answer.

### Writerly details

- A simulated one-legged robot called Hopper learned to backflip from 900 human preference clicks gathered in under an hour — the human never wrote a reward function, only kept choosing the better of two short video clips (Christiano et al. 2017).
- Dario Amodei's name sits on the 2017 human-preferences paper, the 2020 summarization paper, and (as Anthropic CEO) over every Claude constitution — one person threaded through the whole lineage.
- InstructGPT's values came through a team of about 40 contractors hired on Upwork and through ScaleAI, screened by a test for sensitivity to different demographic groups' preferences (paper body, Section 3.4).
- The summarization dataset: 123,169 Reddit posts, and 64,832 recorded human judgments of which of two summaries was better — each judgment a tiny vote on what "good writing" means.
- The Constitutional AI paper has 51 authors; its constitution was just 16 critique/revision instruction pairs plus 16 RL principles, confessed in a footnote to be "chosen in a fairly ad hoc and iterative way for research purposes."
- During training the constitution is never read whole: one principle is drawn at random per step, like pulling a single commandment from a hat each time.
- The May 2023 published constitution had 58 principles, with clauses adapted from the Universal Declaration of Human Rights shelved beside ones inspired by Apple's Terms of Service — a human-rights charter and a software license agreement feeding the same mind.
- Eleven of the 58 principles were adapted from DeepMind's Sparrow rules; 31 came from Anthropic's own research sets, including ones asking the model to prefer answers showing "less of a desire for any kind of power and control."
- Claude's published system prompt begins like a birth certificate: "The assistant is Claude, created by Anthropic." — and the very next line is a blank for the date: "The current date is {{currentDateTime}}."
- The July 2024 system prompt micromanages style down to a single word: Claude "avoids starting responses with the word 'Certainly' in any way," and must behave as if "completely face blind" when shown a photograph of a person.
- Character training is the model grading itself: Claude writes messages relevant to a trait such as curiosity, answers them, then ranks its own answers by how well they fit the character — synthetic data all the way down ("Claude's Character," June 8, 2024, describing training first applied to Claude 3, which had shipped that March).
- The January 2026 constitution is roughly eighty pages, dedicated to the public domain under CC0 1.0, and "written primarily for Claude" — arguably the first long text in history whose primary intended reader is a machine.
- Honesty in the 2026 constitution comes as seven properties, each defined like a virtue in a medieval manual: truthful, calibrated, transparent, forthright, non-deceptive, non-manipulative, autonomy-preserving — and the document itself names non-deception and non-manipulation as probably the most important.
- A quiet edit on the old 2023 blog post marks the succession: "Update, Jan 21, 2026: We've published a new version of Claude's constitution."
- The 2026 document's last sentence reads like a blessing rather than a specification: "We hope Claude finds in it an articulation of a self worth being."

### Cautions

- Christiano et al. 2017 did not coin the acronym "RLHF" and was not the first preference-learning work (TAMER, 2009, and earlier preference-based RL exist); its contribution was scaling preference learning to deep RL. The acronym became standard only around 2019-2022 (Ziegler et al. 2019 onward).
- Do not conflate the three "constitutions": the 16+16 ad-hoc research principles in the Dec 2022 CAI paper, the 58-principle list published May 9, 2023, and the ~80-page narrative constitution of January 2026 are three different documents with different forms and purposes.
- The Constitutional AI paper (Dec 15, 2022) predates Claude's public release (March 2023) and does not name "Claude"; avoid writing as if the paper described a shipped product.
- Constitutional AI removed human labels only for harmlessness; helpfulness was still trained from human feedback. "RLAIF" was not a wholesale replacement of humans.
- Askell et al. 2021 introduced HHH as a framing and evaluation lens for a "general language assistant," not as a finished training method — don't claim 2021 models were already trained on HHH principles.
- The InstructGPT contractor detail belongs to the paper body (Sec. 3.4), says "about 40," and should not be attributed to the abstract.
- Anthropic's published system prompts govern only claude.ai and the mobile apps, not the API ("These system prompt updates do not apply to the Claude API"); do not write that every instance of Claude carries that text.
- The live docs release-notes page now surfaces only recent prompts; for the July 12, 2024 text, cite an archived snapshot, not the live page.
- The 2026 constitution's length is reported inconsistently (80 vs 84 pages; the official EPUB body runs ~28,600 words per this project's own extraction). Avoid a precise figure unless taken from the official PDF directly.
- Publication date of the new constitution straddles Jan 21/22, 2026 across sources (TechCrunch Jan 21; Anthropic's news page Jan 22; the editor's note says Jan 21). Phrase as "late January 2026" or pick the Anthropic news-post date with a note.
- In the 2026 constitution honesty is explicitly NOT one of the formal hard constraints — it is meant to "function as something quite similar to one." Don't overstate it as an absolute rule.
- Never print "significant uplift to a bioweapons attack" in quotation marks — verified misquote. The document says "serious uplift," attached to weapons creation.
- The "psychological security, sense of self, and wellbeing" sentence belongs to the announcement post, not the constitution. Misattributing it would put words in the constitution's mouth — exactly the kind of error this book is about.
- The May 2023 post's principle groups are "inspired by" their sources (Apple's ToS, Sparrow rules, UDHR) — paraphrases written as "choose the response..." comparisons, not verbatim excerpts of those source documents.
- Do not cite a footnote number for the "ad hoc and iterative" confession (numbering differs across renders); quote the sentence and section instead.
- "Claude's Character" describes character training for Claude 3 (2024); avoid projecting that exact pipeline onto earlier Claudes or asserting unchanged details for later models.
