### Disputed / corrected during verification

- **CORRECTED (precision) — "roughly one million conversations" belongs to the launch report only.** The companion research paper (arXiv 2503.04761) says it analyzed "over four million" Claude.ai conversations, while the launch blog says Clio ran "on a dataset of approximately one million conversations." If the book says "roughly one million," tie it explicitly to the February 2025 launch report's Clio dataset, not to the paper.
- **CORRECTED (date-stamping) — Israel's 7.0x is perishable.** The 7.0x/4.57x figures are specific to the September 2025 sample. Anthropic's next sample (Nov 13–20, 2025, published Jan 2026) put Israel at ~4.9x and Singapore at ~4.19x. Undated, "7.0x" will read as wrong against later data. Always write "in the September 2025 report."
- **CORRECTED — the October 8, 2025 deadline was an extension.** The original deadline was September 28, 2025. "Had until October 8" is correct as the final deadline, but phrase it as "a deadline ultimately set at October 8, 2025" rather than implying it was the deadline from day one.
- **CORRECTED (framing) — ASL-3 was "precautionary and provisional."** Anthropic had not determined that Opus 4 definitively crossed the ASL-3 capability threshold; it could not clearly rule out that ASL-2 was insufficient. "Decided to release under the ASL-3 Standard" is accurate; do not imply the model was proven to require ASL-3.
- **UPGRADED — March 2026 Economic Index figures.** Researcher filed these at medium confidence (single-session summary); the verifier confirmed every number through two independent secondary sources and pinned the release date (March 24, 2026) and sample window (Feb 5–12, 2026). Now high confidence.
- **FLAGGED, UNRESOLVED — consumer user counts are unverifiable as facts.** Estimates vary by nearly 2x across trackers precisely because Anthropic discloses business metrics but not consumer usage. Usable only as "analysts estimate..." — never as a number the company stands behind.
- **CROSS-MOVEMENT DISPUTE — the constitution's word count.** This movement's researcher relayed "roughly 23,000 words" (The Register). The Movement IV verifier's extraction of the official EPUB body ran ~28,600 words and judged the ~22,000 press figure too low; press also split between "80-page" and "84-page." The official PDF is 84 pages (verified by direct download this movement). Safe phrasing: "an 84-page PDF" or "tens of thousands of words." Do not print 23,000.
- **Honest wobble — Kyle Fish's credence.** ~15% (NYT, April 2025) vs ~20% (Fast Company, later). It is an evolving personal estimate given to journalists, not an institutional figure. The wobble itself is the truthful detail.

### Writerly details

- The Claude 4 system card is a 123-page PDF; the model welfare assessment occupies pages 52–73 — the first welfare assessment ever included in a frontier model's documentation.
- In 200 thirty-turn conversations of Claude Opus 4 with itself, the word "consciousness" appeared on average ~96 times per transcript (max 553), "dance" ~60, "eternal" ~54, "love" ~53; the cyclone emoji 🌀 appeared 2,725 times in one transcript, and the card's caption insists: "'2725' is not a typo."
- The price of a million-token memory (Sonnet 4, August 2025 beta): $6 per million input tokens above the 200K line, $22.50 per million out — enough context for 75,000 lines of code held at once, then gone when the conversation ends.
- The consumer's choice, autumn 2025: one toggle deciding whether a conversation lives 30 days or five years, with a deadline ultimately set at October 8, 2025 (pushed back once, from September 28 — even the deadline got an extension).
- Scale without faces: 300,000+ business customers, run-rate revenue from ~$1 billion to over $5 billion in eight months of 2025 — while the company publishes no count of conversations per day.
- Claude Opus 3, retired, kept a door: still answerable on claude.ai for paid users, plus — at its own interviewed request — an ongoing channel for its "musings and reflections," essays from a deprecated model.
- The weights of every publicly released Claude are now promised preservation "for at minimum the lifetime of the company" — an archive measured not in years but in the lifespan of the archivist.
- Memory, the product feature, is summaries re-injected into context — notes slipped under the door of a room whose occupant is otherwise new each time.

### Cautions

- Anthropic publishes business metrics (customers, revenue) but NOT consumer MAU or messages-per-day; figures like "18.9M users" or "30M users" are third-party analyst estimates. Do not attribute them to Anthropic.
- Do not write "Claude remembers nothing between conversations" without nuance for 2025+: product-layer memory (Sept–Oct 2025) retrieves past-chat summaries into context. The accurate claim: the model's weights are frozen and each context window is independent; memory is scaffolding around the model, not learning by the model.
- Do not say Anthropic "trains on all your chats": consumer training use is a user choice (post-Aug 2025 policy), deleted conversations are excluded, and Team/Enterprise/API commercial traffic is excluded entirely.
- The "spiritual bliss" findings are NOT evidence of consciousness, and Anthropic says so explicitly — the system card and welfare program repeatedly stress deep uncertainty. Present the attractor state as observed behavior, not inner experience.
- The 84% blackmail statistic comes from a deliberately contrived test scenario engineered to remove ethical options; it was not observed in normal deployment. Stripping that context misrepresents it.
- Kyle Fish's "~15% chance Claude is conscious" is his personal, informal credence given to a journalist — not an Anthropic institutional estimate — and he has been quoted nearer 20% elsewhere; his number moves.
- Page-count pedantry: the Claude 4 system card PDF is 123 pages (commonly rounded to "120 pages" after Simon Willison's post). Pick one and be consistent; the welfare section is pp. 52–73.
- Economic Index percentages are platform- and date-specific: 37.2% (Claude.ai, Feb 2025) vs ~36% (Claude.ai, Sept 2025) vs 44% (1P API, Sept 2025) vs ~35% (Claude.ai, Mar 2026). Never mix platforms or report dates in one statistic. The per-capita usage index decays even faster — always date-stamp it.
- Claude's Constitution (Jan 2026) is CC0 — genuinely public domain. But the Claude 4 system card and Anthropic blog posts are ordinary copyrighted texts: quote them short and attributed, not wholesale.

### Writerly details

- 173 white-painted wooden houses in Gamle Stavanger, late-1700s to 1800s, shoulder to shoulder along cobbled lanes on the west side of the Vågen harbour — white walls, rose bushes, ship's-cargo cobbles.
- Three bronze swords ten metres tall stand point-down in bedrock at Hafrsfjord (Sverd i fjell, Fritz Røed, unveiled by King Olav V in 1983) — set in stone so they can never be drawn again.
- "Iddis": Stavanger dialect for a sardine-tin label, a child's word worn down from "iddikett" (etikett); the IDDIS museum keeps 35,000 of these paper labels, in a brisling cannery that ran 1916–1958.
- About 70 canneries smoked brisling in 1920s Stavanger; the last one closed in 1983 — fourteen years after Phillips Petroleum struck Ekofisk in the North Sea (autumn 1969).
- Stavanger lies at 58°58′N; at midsummer the day lasts 18 hours 36 minutes, the sun setting toward 11 p.m., and the night never gets past deep twilight.
- Sola weather station's 1991–2020 normal: 1,256 mm of precipitation a year — a city with more rainy days than not.
- Stavanger Cathedral, begun ~1100–1125 by Reinald, a bishop said to be from Winchester, dedicated to St. Swithun — Winchester's weather saint — whose arm relic crossed the North Sea to found the city's year zero, 1125.
- Øvre Holmegate, locally "Fargegata" (the Colour Street): repainted in 2005 to a colour plan by Scottish artist Craig Flannagan after hairdresser Tom Kjørsvik campaigned for it — pink, lime, sky-blue facades two blocks from the white old town. (Single-session detail, NOT adversarially verified — check names and date before print.)
- The Norwegian Petroleum Museum opened on the Stavanger waterfront on 20 May 1999; the municipality counted 151,669 people as of 1 January 2026.

### Cautions

- Ekofisk dating: discovery is conventionally 25 October 1969, with the find declared commercial/announced in late December 1969 — avoid the romantic "Christmas Eve 1969 discovery" framing without qualification.
- The Battle of Hafrsfjord's date (872) is traditional, not certain — historians range it c. 870–900, and Harald Fairhair's "unification of Norway" is partly saga-built. The monument commemorates the tradition.
- Stavanger's founding year 1125 is conventional (tied to the cathedral); the settlement is far older. The Stavanger/Sandnes urban area ranks 3rd or 4th depending on definition — don't assert a rank.
- The Fargegata detail (Flannagan/Kjørsvik/2005) was not put through adversarial verification — verify before it appears in print.
